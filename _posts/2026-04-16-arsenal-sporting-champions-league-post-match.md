**Arsenal 0-0 Sporting CP** (agg. **1-0**). The Emirates second leg finished **goalless**, which is enough: **Kai Havertz**’s late winner in Portugal eight days earlier remains the **whole tie** in one swing. Arsenal advance to a **Champions League semi-final** against **Atlético Madrid**—back-to-back final-four runs, with the hard part of this quarter-final already banked before kickoff in north London.

## What kind of game was it?

The honest answer is **thin on clear chances** and **heavy on tension**. Sporting, chasing history of their own, carried stretches of the **first half**; Arsenal pressed with intent early but struggled to turn territory into **Rui Silva** tests. After the break the match briefly opened—**Noni Madueke** cut inside and clipped the **side netting** in the opening phase of the half—but the pattern soon reverted: Sporting wary of conceding, Arsenal protecting an aggregate that **flipped the risk** in their favor.

Both sides **struck the woodwork** either side of half-time; a **Geny Catamo** volley in particular forced a collective intake of breath. For long stretches the keepers were spectators until the game sharpened late.

## The moments that mattered

With ten minutes left, a **Declan Rice** corner dropped awkwardly and **Leandro Trossard** met it with a header from a tight angle—**off the post**, inches from sealing it in the match itself. That would have been the exclamation point; instead, the night stayed **0-0**.

The last word almost belonged to **Sporting**. After a long throw and a scramble, **João Simões** struck from the edge of the box deep in stoppage time—the ball **flew past the wrong side of the netting** from an Arsenal perspective, close enough that **relief** was the only sensible emotion. No goal; **no away-goal heartbreak**.

## The numbers (broad strokes)

Roughly **even possession** (~50–50). Arsenal **out-shot** Sporting (**15** to **8**) but both sides managed only **one shot on target** apiece—this was never a shootout. **Corners** stacked in Arsenal’s favor (**8–3**), but quality over quantity never quite arrived.

## Reading it against the preview

The [first-leg preview]({% post_url 2026-04-06-sporting-arsenal-preview-camavinga %}) framed **control** in Lisbon; the [second-leg preview]({% post_url 2026-04-15-arsenal-sporting-cp-champions-league-second-leg %}) worried about the **in-between** state: control without incision, and the **knife-fight** math if Sporting landed a punch. We got something closer to **professional ugliness**: not a performance to frame on a wall, but a **result** that matches the **aggregate** story. Lisbon did the attacking work; London did the **closing**.

## What’s next

**Atlético** in the semi-finals—and before that, the league calendar keeps rolling, including a **Manchester City** test that will demand a different kind of control than this **European night** required.

Progression **counts**. The football, on this occasion, was **secondary**.
