Arsenal welcome **Sporting CP** to the **Emirates** with a **1-0 aggregate lead** from the first leg in Lisbon—a late **Kai Havertz** goal changed the complexion of the tie, but it is still the kind of margin that keeps the door open for the visitors. Sandwiched between a bruising spell in the league and a daunting trip to **Manchester City** next weekend, this is the night where Arteta’s side either bank progression or invite another spring of **European regret**.

The emotional contrast matters. At home, Arsenal are expected to **attack the game** more than they did in Portugal, yet the same structural tension remains: push too recklessly and Sporting’s transitions—especially through wide runners and a mobile front line—can punish you in a single sequence.

## Predicted line-up

**Front three:** Martinelli – Havertz – Saka  

**Midfield:** Rice – Zubimendi – Ødegaard  

**Defense:** Timber – Gabriel – Saliba – White  

**Goalkeeper:** Raya  

Fitness clouds hang over several names (**Ødegaard**, **Saka**, **Timber**, **Calafiori**, **Merino** have all been in the “wait and see” bucket in the build-up), so this XI is partly **best-case**. If minutes need managing, **Trossard** and **Eze** become natural levers without changing the overall shape.

Tactically, the watchpoint is similar to the away leg: **Ben White**’s **inverted** instincts can compress the midfield, but they can also leave **exposure** if Sporting break with speed down his side. At the Emirates, the trade-off tilts toward **risk-taking**—just not the chaotic kind.

## Game outlook

A one-goal lead is **not** a license to sit deep for ninety minutes. Sporting can afford to be patient: one away goal shifts the arithmetic, and from there the tie becomes a **knife fight**.

Two plausible scripts:

1. **Sporting** land the first big chance on the break, the crowd tightens, and Arsenal spend the second half chasing **calm** as much as goals.  
2. **Arsenal** score early, force Sporting to stretch, and the match opens into the type of **controlled dominance** Arteta’s team is built for—second balls, set plays, and sustained wide overloads.

What you hope to avoid is the **in-between** state: plenty of possession, few clear openings, and a growing sense that one mistake could undo a season’s European work.

## Camavinga rumours: still relevant?

Links to **Eduardo Camavinga** remain **speculative**, but the profile still maps onto Arsenal’s long-term midfield puzzle: **versatility**, ball-carrying, and minutes across a **six** or **eight** in a heavy schedule.

As before, the hierarchy of needs may push midfield down the list if the club prioritizes **wide attacking** reinforcement. If elite targets move early elsewhere, Camavinga becomes the sort of name that can **escalate quickly** in late-window momentum.

## Looking ahead: summer transfer window

The broad summer picture is unchanged in outline.

### 1. Left wing reinforcement

Names like **Kvaratskhelia** and **Anthony Gordon** will continue to dominate conversation; if those paths stall, **Ndiaye**-type profiles tend to emerge as more **pragmatic** solutions.

### 2. Midfield depth

Rotation and injury insurance around **Rice** and **Zubimendi** matter—**Tonali**, **Anderson**, and **Camavinga** sit in different tiers of feasibility, not one interchangeable bucket.

### 3. Right back

If **Ben White** moves on, **Kayode** and **Livramento** are the sort of links that fit Arsenal’s age profile and athletic demands.

## Final thoughts

This is no longer about **surviving** an away leg. It is about **finishing** a quarter-final at home: score when chances come, manage the middle third when the game wobbles, and do not let **nervous energy** become a **tactical** problem.

Win the night, and the season’s center of gravity shifts back toward belief. Slip, and the Bournemouth aftertaste becomes the headline nobody wants.
