This is a **research paper** I wrote as a student at **Qibao Dwight High School** (Shanghai). It was **published** in *BCP Business & Management* (vol. 32, pp. 257–264, **22 November 2022**), in an issue the publisher groups with the **AFTEM 2022** event. The piece **reviews and synthesizes** existing writing and news—it does **not** report new data or econometric estimates. I share it here as a straightforward record of **early student work**, separate from what I do now in college.

## What the paper does

Using **secondary sources** (research summaries, policy commentary, and news), the paper discusses possible **economic effects** of the Biden administration’s **“Build Back Better”** agenda and sketches **optimization ideas** (e.g. themes around **inflation targeting**, **interest-rate** context, and **input-subsidy** style trade-offs). The goal was to organize debates about a large fiscal package during the **pandemic recovery**, not to estimate a formal model.
